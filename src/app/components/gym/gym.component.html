gym.

gym is not only lifting weights and building big muscles. there's a lot of stuff and workouts you can do at the gym, but here I will show you what I did, and I'm still doing, for building muscles.

FLAT BENCH PRESS

One of the best exercises that you can do for building upper body strength. This is a MUST DO exercise.

Stick your chest out, grab the bar where it feels the most comfortable, adjust your legs so your body is stable at all times. Make sure you control the movement at all times.

MUSCLES: Chest, Triceps, Shoulders

DIFFICULTY: Easy

INCLINE BENCH PRESS

Harder variation of bench press that targets more of your upper chest. Be careful with the weight you're lifting since you're weaker than on flat bench press.

Stick your chest out, grab the bar where it feels the most comfortable, adjust your legs so your body is stable at all times. Make sure you control the movement at all times.

MUSCLES: Upper chest, Triceps, Shoulders

DIFFICULTY: Medium

OVERHEAD PRESS

Great exercise for building upper body strenght that targets more of your shoulders. This is a MUST DO exercise.

Grab the bar a bit narrower than on benchpress, don't let your elbows go wide and make sure the bar doesn't go below your upper chest.

MUSCLES: Shoulders, Triceps, Upper chest

DIFFICULTY: Easy

SHOULDER FLY

An exercise that targets your side delts and gives your shoulders that round shape. You can't lift too much weight with this exercise so be careful which dumbbels you pick.

Grab your dumbbels and lift them with your shoulders to the side. Make sure you don't swing your body.

MUSCLES: Shoulders

DIFFICULTY: Easy

WEIGHTED DIPS

Alongside benchpress, one of the best exercises you can do to increase your upper body strength. This is a MUST DO exercise.

Lean forward and tighten your core. Make sure you do full range of motion.

MUSCLES: Chest, Triceps, Shoulders.

DIFFICULTY: Medium

INCLINE CHEST FLY

A great exercise that targets your upper chest. Be careful with the weights, since your shoulders are under a lot of pressure.

Stick your chest out, place your legs in a way that will make your body stable and don't go too low with the dumbbells.

MUSCLES: Upper chest, shoulders.

DIFFICULTY: Easy

BENT OVER ROWS

Great exercise for building a wide back. Be careful with how much weight you put because your lower back is under a lot of pressure.

Grab a bar a bit wider than your shoulder width, bend over and try to be as parallel as you can with the floor and try not to use momentum from your legs.

MUSCLES: Back, Biceps, Forearms

DIFFICULTY: Medium

ONE ARM ROWS

A back isolation exercise that hits your latissimus muscle even more.

Bend over or use a bench to get into a position like in a video, grab a dumbbell with your thumb over the bar and try not to use too much momentum.

MUSCLES: Back, Biceps, Forearms

DIFFICULTY: Easy

BICEPS CURLS

A workout finisher that gives you a great biceps pump. Should be done at the end of your workout.

Grab a bar where your hands are as wide as your shoulders and go all the way up and down. Don't use too much momentum. For the last 2-3 reps you can throw the weights up and do negative reps.

MUSCLES: Biceps, Forearms.

DIFFICULTY: Easy

SQUATS

One of the best exercises that you can do for your whole body, but primarily for your legs. This is a MUST DO exercise.

Try to figure out if low or high bar placement on your back suits you better. Your legs should be at your shoulder width and feet should be facing out a bit. Tighten your core so you lower back doesn't hurt and do the exercise slow and controlled.

MUSCLES: Legs, back.

DIFFICULTY: Medium

ROMANIAN DEADLIFT

An exercise that targets your hamstrings and glute muscles. Should be done with squats to build complete legs.

Your legs should be at your shoulder width, push your hips back but don't move your knees forward. Lift with your legs, not with your back. Tighten your core so your lower back doesn't hurt.

MUSCLES: Legs, back.

DIFFICULTY: Medium

CONVENTIONAL DEADLIFT

A harder variation of deadlifts. Great for your whole body strength.

Your knees should be at your shoulder width, squat down with your legs and keep your back straight. Lift the barbell with your legs not with your back. Tighten your core so your lower back doesn't hurt.

MUSCLES: Legs, back, arms.

DIFFICULTY: Medium

SPLIT SQUAT

A harder variation of squats. Be careful with the weights because you can lose your balance.

Step out with your your leg, tighten your core so your lower back doesn't hurt and carefully perform the exercise.

MUSCLES: Legs, back.

DIFFICULTY: Hard
