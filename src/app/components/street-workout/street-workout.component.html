street workout.

street workout is a workout style in a park or on a street, wherever you can find bars. exercises are done with your own body and range from very easy ones that everyone can do to very hard ones that only experiences athletes can do and beginners can only admire.

PULLUPS

Great exercise for building upper body strength that targets back and arm muscles. This is a MUST DO exercise.

Palms are facing away from you, retract your back, tighten your core and legs and pull yourself up.

MUSCLES: Back, Biceps, Forearms

DIFFICULTY: Moderate

INVERTED ROWS

The easiest variation of pulling exercises. Great for beginners, super sets and warming up.

Find a low bar that suits your strength. The lower it is the harder the exercise. You can make it easier by bending your knees. Try to put a thumb over the bar and pull with your back, not with your hands.

MUSCLES: Back, Biceps, Forearms

DIFFICULTY: Easy

CLOSE GRIP PULLUPS

This exercise puts more focus on your biceps, but your back is still a primary muslce group. You can target your biceps even more with your palms facing you.

Palms are facing away from you, retract your back, tighten your core and legs and pull yourself up.

MUSCLES: Back, Biceps, Forearms

DIFFICULTY: Moderate

ALTERNATING PULLUPS

If you don't have a monkey bar or gymnastic rings, you can do this exercise instead, but neutral grip pullups, especially wide variation, is a way better option for building a good back.

Grab a bar with opposite grips, one hand in front of the other, retract your back muscles, tighten your core and legs and pull yourself up side to side.

MUSCLES: Back, Biceps, Forearms

DIFFICULTY: Moderate

CHINUPS

A pullup variation where your palms are facing you is called a chinup. This exercise targets more of your biceps, a bit less than a close grip pullup. This is a MUST DO exercise. This is a pullup variation where you are the strongest.

Palms are facing you, retract your back, tighten your core and legs and pull yourself up.

MUSCLES: Back, Biceps, Forearms

DIFFICULTY: Moderate

CLOSE GRIP CHINUPS

This is a workout finisher. Your biceps are, with your back, working the most here. Do this at the end of your workout.

Palms are facing you, retract your back, tighten your core and legs and pull yourself up.

MUSCLES: Back, Biceps, Forearms

DIFFICULTY: Moderate

NEUTRAL PULLUPS

This is what some people call a perfect pullup. The load is balanced between your back and arm muscles. This is a MUST DO exercise.

Palms are facing opposite of each other, retract your back, tighten your core and legs and pull yourself up.

MUSCLES: Back, Biceps, Forearms

DIFFICULTY: Moderate

WIDE NEUTRAL PULLUPS

Harder variation of neutral pullups, but great for building a wide back. If you can, do this instead of regular neutral pullups.

Palms are facing opposite of each other, retract your back, tighten your core and legs and pull yourself up.

MUSCLES: Back, Biceps, Forearms

DIFFICULTY: Moderate

DIPS

They say what squats are for legs, dips are for triceps. One of the best upper body exercises, alongside pullups, that you can do. This is a MUST DO exercise.

Find bars that are not too wide or too narrow, tighten your core, put your legs in front of you, bend over a bit and never change your form during the exercise. Your body has to stay tight at all times.

MUSCLES: Triceps, Lower chest, Front shoulder

DIFFICULTY: Moderate

STRAIGHT BAR DIPS

Harder variation of dips. This variation puts more stress on your shoulders and triceps. Since your front shoulder and triceps are working in every pushing exercise, you should stick to regular dips, since they target more of your chest muscles.

Grab a bar a bit wider than your sholder length, tighten your core, put your legs in front, bend over a bit and stay stable during the exercise.

MUSCLES: Triceps, Lower chest, Front shoulder

DIFFICULTY: Moderate

PUSHUPS

In my opinion the best pushing exercise after dips. If you can do a lot of pushups do them at the end of your workout. There are a lot of variations, but this regular pushups will do just fine. This is a MUST DO exercise.

Put your hands a bit wider than your shoulder length, tighten your core and legs and stay stable during the exercise.

MUSCLES: Triceps, Chest, Front shoulder

DIFFICULTY: Easy

SKULL-CRUSHERS

A workout finisher. Targets the long (inside) head of your triceps that is hard to target and makes your arm look big. This is a MUST DO exercise.

Grab a bar as narrow as you can, tighten your whole body and don't push yourself with your legs.

MUSCLES: Triceps

DIFFICULTY: Easy

MUSCLE UP

A show-off exercise. Doesn't really build muscle or require strength. It takes time to learn the technique.

Get into pullup position, swing yourself forward, pull yourself up with back muscles, swing your legs and lean forward at the top.

MUSCLES: Back, Triceps

DIFFICULTY: Hard
